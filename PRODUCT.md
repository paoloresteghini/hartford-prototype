# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

B2B decision-makers only: event producers, production managers, corporate AV coordinators, school-district IT buyers. They arrive knowing roughly what gear class they need (e.g. "PTZ cameras for a general session"), browse by category → subcategory (search is secondary), and their job is to assemble a quote request — pricing is never displayed. They are often on-site or mobile, under deadline.

## Product Purpose

POC redesign of hartfordrents.com (Hartford Technology Rentals — top-5 US AV & computing rental house, Las Vegas / Arlington Heights / Orlando, nationwide delivery). Scope: homepage, one category page (Camera Rentals), one product detail page (Panasonic AK-UC4000). Goal: sell the client on a full redesign. Success = the client sees a $100k-studio-grade site that preserves their SEO strength (semantic HTML + rich schema.org on every page) and converts browsing into quote requests.

## Brand Personality

Precise, engineered, broadcast-grade. The world of this brand is matte-black flight cases, calibrated lenses, ON-AIR tally lights. Voice: confident technical authority, zero hype. Emotional goal: "these people run million-dollar broadcasts; my event is safe with them."

## Anti-references

- Current hartfordrents.com: dated WooCommerce theme, loud red everywhere, cluttered.
- Generic AI landing pages: cream backgrounds, gradient text, icon-card grids, eyebrow labels on every section.
- Consumer rental sites (BorrowLenses-style price-first grids) — this is B2B, no pricing, quote-first.
- Editorial-magazine aesthetic (display serifs, italics) — wrong register for broadcast engineering.

## Design Principles

1. **Catalog is the hero.** Navigation through categories/subcategories (Lowes-style mega menu) is the primary UX; every page is a doorway deeper into the fleet.
2. **Quote List replaces cart.** No prices anywhere; the entire conversion flow is "add to quote → send request." Make that flow feel effortless and ever-present.
3. **Spec-sheet honesty.** Real model numbers, real specs, mono-set data. Technical buyers trust density done cleanly.
4. **Red means ON AIR.** The logo red survives only as a small signal color (tally dot, active states, quote badge) on a monochrome graphite/white system.
5. **SEO is structure.** Semantic landmarks, one h1 per page, breadcrumbs, JSON-LD (Organization, WebSite, CollectionPage/ItemList, Product, FAQPage, BreadcrumbList) on all pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA: 4.5:1 body contrast, visible focus states, full keyboard support for mega menu and quote drawer, prefers-reduced-motion alternatives for all animation, touch targets ≥44px on mobile.
